Omega-3 fish oil and krill oil supplements are one of the few additions almost any diet can benefit from. Research consistently ties these fats to heart health, brain function, and joint comfort. But the supplement aisle is crowded, and prices vary wildly, often for reasons that have little to do with quality. The key is focusing on EPA and DHA content per serving, the form of the oil (triglyceride forms tend to absorb better), and freshness indicators like the bottle size and return policy. Budget-friendly doesn’t mean cutting corners. The best value supplements pack a meaningful dose without expensive branding or exotic oils. A few smart trade-offs, like choosing a slightly larger softgel or a less fancy capsule color, can save you serious money while still delivering the same clinically studied benefits.
